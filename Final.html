More Guns, More Violence?

By Ruobing Su

U.S. Major mass shooting since 1982

The deadly school shooting in Parkland in Feburary, has ignited national outrage and calls for action on gun reform. Since 1982, there have been at least 98 public mass shootings across the country, with the killings unfolding in 34 states from Massachusetts to Hawaii; fifty-nine of these mass shootings have occurred since 2006. Although, the definition of mass shooting varies place to place, according to FBI, mass shooting is a wide range of gun crimes in which four or more people have been either wounded or killed.

The numbers are starggering.

Even though, mass shooting can be differently defined based on fatalities and injuries, the number of mass shooting is starggering as year goes by, in particular after 2010. Over 48% of mass shooting recorded by Mother Jones took place after 2010.

However, it is not the number of mass shooting cases increasing exponetially in recent years - people who are involved in mass shooting, namely, the number of people fatalities and injuries, is also increasing. The 2017 Las Vegas shooting is considered as the deadliest mass shooting committed by an individual in U.S. history, leaving 58 dead and over 600 people injured.

Why AR-15 keeps appearing in U.S. mass shootings?

AR-15-style rifles have increasingly appeared in American mass shootings, including the deadliest high school shooting in the nation's history at Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School in Parkland, Florida.

Semi-automatic weapons are most frequently used in mass shooting by the killers. More than often, the killers have multiple weapons for planned or unplanned shootings. Modified weapons, such as automatic rifles which are being used in Las Vegas shooting, are relatively rare while more deadly.
